Publications

FEEL (Force-Enhanced Egocentric Learning): A Dataset for Physical Action Understanding

Eadom Dessalene, Botao He, Michael Maynord, Yonatan Tussa, Pavan Mantripragada, Yianni Karabatis, Nirupam Roy, Yiannis Aloimonos

FEEL is the first large-scale egocentric dataset pairing video with synchronized force measurements from custom piezoresistive gloves. The dataset contains approximately 3 million force-synchronized frames of natural, unscripted kitchen manipulation and introduces force as a physically grounded supervisory signal for contact understanding and action representation learning.

Monocular Reconstruction of Neural Tactile Fields

Pavan Mantripragada, Siddhanth Deshmukh, Eadom Dessalene, Manas Desai, Yiannis Aloimonos

Robots must reason about how objects respond to contact, such as what can be pushed or deformed, yet this interaction knowledge is missing from vision-based perception. To address this, we leverage real sensor data to predict a neural representation of physical response directly from images. This enables real-to-sim transfer and supports robotics tasks such as interaction-aware manipulation.

EmbodiSwap for Zero-Shot Robot Imitation Learning

Eadom Dessalene*, Pavan Mantripragada*, Michael Maynord, Yiannis Aloimonos

We introduce EmbodiSwap, a method for producing photorealistic synthetic robot overlays on human video. The approach helps bridge the embodiment gap between in-the-wild egocentric human videos and a target robot embodiment, enabling zero-shot imitation learning for robot manipulation.

Extremum Seeking Controlled Wiggling for Tactile Insertion

Levi Burner*, Pavan Mantripragada*, Gabriele M. Caddeo*, Lorenzo Natale, Cornelia Fermüller, Yiannis Aloimonos

When humans perform complex insertion tasks such as pushing a cup into a cupboard, routing a cable, or putting a key in a lock, they wiggle the object and adapt the process through tactile feedback. We study an extremum seeking control law that wiggles end effector pose to maximize insertion depth while minimizing strain measured by a GelSight Mini sensor. Across key insertion and assembly tasks, the method significantly outperforms a CMA-ES baseline and achieves strong vision-initialized performance without contact modeling or learning.

AcTExplore: Active Tactile Exploration on Unknown Objects

Amir-Hossein Shahidzadeh, Seong Jong Yoo, Pavan Mantripragada, Chahat Deep Singh, Cornelia Fermüller, Yiannis Aloimonos

Tactile exploration plays a crucial role in understanding object structures for robotics tasks such as grasping and manipulation, but efficiently exploring unknown objects with limited sensing coverage is challenging. AcTExplore is an active tactile exploration method driven by reinforcement learning that incrementally collects tactile data and reconstructs 3D object shapes in a limited number of steps. The method achieves an average of 95.97% IoU coverage on unseen YCB objects while being trained only on primitive shapes.

GradTac: Spatio-Temporal Gradient Based Tactile Sensing

Kanishka Ganguly, Pavan Mantripragada, Chethan M. Parameshwara, Cornelia Fermüller, Nitin J. Sanket, Yiannis Aloimonos

GradTac introduces a spatio-temporal gradient representation for data from fluid-based tactile sensors, inspired by neuromorphic event-based sensing. The method converts discrete tactile measurements into spatio-temporal surfaces and tracks tactile contours across them, improving robustness to noise and enabling accurate tracking of touch regions. It is evaluated on real-world experiments with the Shadow Dexterous Hand and BioTac SP sensors across tasks including force estimation, slip detection, and edge tracking.
